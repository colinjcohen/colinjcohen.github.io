chapter six: the woman in the yellow shawl

 

 

THE SUN CRAWLED above the streets of Josefov.

A woman in a yellow shawl came upon the damp cobblestones of Červená Street. Having reached the intersection of it and Maisel.

Years later, she would tell me how she had stopped cold, her mouth agape. The door to the High Synagogue was open.

She sped into the building. She slammed and locked the door. She checked around.

Beside an old, arched door was the Luger that the young SS officer in his fright had dropped the night before.

Despite the gun’s incongruity in the synagogue, she zeroed in not at it. But at the door.

Grabbing the gun, she placed it in her purse.

From this she withdrew an iron key hundreds of years old. She used it to unlock the door.

With a drawn-out breath, she pushed the door.

It screeched open.

The woman’s eyes locked onto the nothingness in front of her. She returned the key. She took out a flashlight.

Flicking it on, she strode down the ancient stone steps.
