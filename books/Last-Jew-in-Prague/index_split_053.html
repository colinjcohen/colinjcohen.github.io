chapter fifty-three: from deep inside the building

 

 

HERMANN STOOD OUTSIDE the Old-New Synagogue’s windows. With the afternoon fading behind him.

Unlit chandeliers were hanging in the 700-year-old prayer hall. The Torah was missing from its ark.

Klaus bent over Hermann’s shoulder. “Do you see them?”

“They’ve probably gone down to the cellar.”

Hermann crept to the synagogue’s front door.

Klaus was in back of him. So close in back of him was he that he was climbing over Hermann’s shoes.

A roar let out. From deep inside the building.

It froze both men.

Klaus grabbed Hermann’s arms. “What was that?”

Hermann took his time answering. The sound had roused a distant memory. “I’m not sure.”

He seized the door’s handle. He pulled it.

The door screeched open. The two took a step into the synagogue.

Hermann switched on the lights.

The men went in search of an arched door that they had no doubt was there.

This wasn’t difficult. The synagogue was smaller than the others. The door jumped out at them from the back of a hallway.

It was locked.

Klaus ran his palm over the door’s surface. “We could get another battering ram.”

Hermann shook his head. “It would take too long.”

He rushed off. To a place that they had passed. “I bet it won’t be necessary.”

Klaus went with him. “Where are you going?”

Hermann stopped by a door on the wall. “Remember when we were in the Pinkas Synagogue cellar the other night? There was a dumbwaiter like this.”

He took the flashlight from Klaus. He opened the door. He lifted himself into the cabinet.

It creaked.

Klaus didn’t bother hiding his concern. “You’re not going down in that, are you?”

“Why not?” Hermann said, appreciating the man’s worry. “Thanks to the Reich putting me on a strict diet regimen, I should be plenty light enough.”

“And if you’re not?”

“I’ll get there faster.”

Hermann crawled into a fetal position. “If I’m in a single piece afterward, I’ll meet you by the cellar door.”
