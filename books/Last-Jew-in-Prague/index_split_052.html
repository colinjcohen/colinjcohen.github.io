chapter fifty-two: a sense of something familiar

 

 

LUDĚK’S TRUCK STAMMERED from the warehouse in the faltering afternoon.

It made its way down Plzeňská Street. It billowed more smoke.

Following it a good ways back were Hermann and Klaus. They followed it to the other side of town. Over what had been Palacký Bridge and was now Mozart Bridge. They followed it northward. To the ghost town. A destination that couldn’t have shocked either man.

The truck parked on U Starého Hřbitova. It was halfway from the Old-New Synagogue above it and the High Synagogue below.

Parking streets behind it were Hermann and Klaus.

They sat in their car waiting. They waited awhile. Without any movement from the truck.

Klaus couldn’t shake off his anxiousness. “What are they doing?”

Hermann remained calm. He projected this onto Klaus. “I’m more interested in what they will be doing and where they’ll be doing it.”

Tonda and Luděk exited the truck. They headed to the rear of it. They stepped into it.

They spent greater time in it than they had in front. They took their time leaving it too.

Along with themselves, they took out a heavy, covered cart with old and creaky wheels. Luděk rolled it toward the Old-New Synagogue.

Klaus lurched forward. His head smacked against the windshield. “What could be in that?”

Hermann gripped his door handle. “We’ll discover soon enough.”

Tonda unlocked and opened the synagogue. He and Luděk entered it with the cart. Tonda closed the door behind them.

Hermann stormed out. He started toward the synagogue. “Bring the flashlight.”

Klaus snagged the device. He left the car.

But he halted. Some familiar vision might have caught his eyes. Or it could’ve been a scent in the air.

Whatever it was, it had scared him.

Hermann took a step toward him. “What’s wrong?”

“Nothing,” Klaus said.

He was convincing himself of this.
