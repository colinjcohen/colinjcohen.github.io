chapter two: when a predator becomes prey

 

 

SOME EVENTS IN this story happened outside of what my grandpa experienced. Some he would piece together afterward. Others I would discover years later from those involved.

This was a latter.

V Praze je klid.

Prague is calm.

The authorities would declare this during the city’s occupation. To create a sense of normalcy.

On this night, it was true.

A fog descended upon the center of Prague in the halting rain. It engulfed the evening’s stillness. It created a scene that you couldn’t place in a particular time.

Breaking this was a gunshot. It echoed off the ancient buildings.

The door to the High Synagogue swung open. A young SS officer burst outside unarmed. The fear on the man’s face was palpable in the varicolored light.

He ran eastward along the cobblestones of Červená Street. He flailed his arms and himself down the narrow road.

A pair of footsteps joined his. They were heavy. They were methodical. They were getting louder.

The soldier ran faster. He didn’t turn back.
