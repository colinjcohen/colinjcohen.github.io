chapter thirty-four: the eyes of a golem

 

 

HERMANN RIFLED THROUGH the belongings of Captain Gruber. In a single-room flat on the first floor of a crumbling building.

The irony of a Jew searching an SS man’s goods wasn’t lost on him. He was reveling in it.

Klaus slid beside him. “I suppose you have no idea what you’re looking for.”

“You’re getting the hang of this.”

Klaus smiled.

Which Hermann liked. He liked the man next to him more than he wanted to admit.

“Call out if I can be of help,” Klaus said.

Hermann didn’t call out. He kept rifling. Through the furniture and the closets and the kitchen drawers. He rifled everywhere.

But was getting nowhere.

So, with a brief but heavy sigh, he turned to Klaus. “You just may be able to assist me, considering that you and Captain Gruber shared the same profession. That you had similar backgrounds and training. If you were him and were hiding some item, where would you do it?”

Klaus wheeled around the room.

He pointed to the bureau by the bed. “I’d use a secret slot in that.”

Hermann sprinted to the bureau. He tossed out the clothes from its drawers.

Kneeling on the floor, he felt around the drawers’ insides. For a latch. Or a similar device.

But he couldn’t come upon any.

So he knocked on the wood in several places. Both inside the bureau and out. Checking for hollowness.

Discovering this in the middle drawer’s bottom, he yanked out the drawer. He felt around it.

“What are you doing?” Klaus asked.

“I can’t figure out how to open it.”

“Let me try.”

Hermann handed the drawer to Klaus.

He broke it open with his fist.

“That’s one way of doing it,” Hermann said.

Klaus handed him the drawer’s pieces. “It’s the surest way.”

Hermann tossed away the broken wood. A leather-bound book was below it.

Klaus’s eyes stretched. “What is it?”

Hermann opened the book. He read its opening paragraphs.

What he had read surprised him. It got him to flip through the pages.

He stopped in multiple places. He read sections of it.

“What is it?” Klaus cried.

“Pornography. And not even good pornography.”

Yet, for a reason Hermann couldn’t explain to himself, he continued flipping. He kept reading.

Reaching the inside back cover, he became shocked.

Klaus tilted over Hermann’s shoulder. Toward an ink drawing of a monstrous golem.

It had blank eyes and the Hebrew word for “truth” carved in its forehead.

“Could he have drawn that from experience?” Klaus stammered.

Hermann didn’t reply. He tossed the book. Onto the shards of wood on the floor.
